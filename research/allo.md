# Allo — Competitor Research

**Funding:** $12M seed round (Oct 2025) — Base10 Partners and Lightspeed Venture Partners  
**HQ:** Europe (EU-focused, not UK-specific)  
**Website:** withallo.com

---

## What the product is

Allo is a mobile-first AI business phone system. The entire product is built around the phone — not a web portal with a mobile app bolted on. It is designed for small teams and sole operators whose business lives in their pocket.

Core features:
- AI answers missed and declined calls automatically
- Voicemail transcription to text (92% accuracy in independent testing)
- Smart call routing — directs calls to the right person or handles them fully (87% accuracy)
- Push notifications within 5 seconds of a call ending
- Business phone number (separate from personal mobile)
- AI call summaries delivered to the owner after each handled call
- Scheduling and appointment booking via AI
- Starts at €14/month

The mobile app IS the product. There is no meaningful desktop portal — configuration, call management, and live data all live in the app.

---

## Who it is for

Small teams and solo operators — the framing is "simple, powerful software for small teams." It is not trades-specific or UK-specific. The target appears to be any small business owner or freelancer who needs a professional phone presence without a receptionist.

Primary use case: the owner is busy or unavailable, AI handles the call, owner gets a notification with a summary and the caller's number.

## Strategic read

Allo validates the mobile-first AI receptionist market at venture scale. The $12M raise from Lightspeed (top-tier global VC) means serious investors believe this category is real and growing.

Allo's weakness is breadth — it is generic and shallow. Qerxel's depth (Q modes, support calls, the coaching panel, element architecture, sector-specific knowledge) is the differentiator. Allo wins on simplicity. Qerxel wins on intelligence.

The risk: if Allo expands aggressively into UK trades with a mobile-first product, they compete directly. The counter: UK sole traders want a product built for them, not adapted for them.
