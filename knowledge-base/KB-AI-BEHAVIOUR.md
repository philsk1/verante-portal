# AI Behaviour

## What is Triage Mode and which one should I choose?

Triage Mode sets Q's overall approach to handling calls. There are three options:

**Balanced** — Q is warm and professional, gathers information, offers sensible options, and closes calls tidily. This is the right choice for most businesses. Q is helpful without being indulgent, and efficient without being cold.

**Thorough** — Q takes more time with each caller, explores their needs more deeply, and ensures nothing is missed before closing. Best for businesses where missing detail is costly — legal, financial, or complex trade enquiries where a misunderstood job could waste time and money.

**Lean** — Q gets to the point quickly. Short answers, fast close, minimal pleasantries. Best for high-volume businesses where call time matters more than conversation depth, or where callers are professionals who know exactly what they want.

When in doubt, start with Balanced. You can always change it after listening to a few calls.

## What is the Tone Register and how does it affect Q?

Tone Register controls the language style Q uses throughout the conversation.

**Warm** — Natural, conversational, friendly. Q speaks the way a trusted local business owner would speak. This works for most trades, beauty, health, and service businesses.

**Formal** — Professional and measured. Q uses complete sentences, avoids colloquialisms, and addresses callers with more ceremony. Best for legal, financial, or professional services where a casual tone would seem out of place.

The tone register affects not just individual phrases but the whole shape of the conversation — how Q opens, how it takes details, how it closes. Set it once and Q maintains it consistently across every call.

## What is the Additional Instructions field?

Additional Instructions is where you tell Q anything specific to your business that does not fit into any other field. Q reads this on every call and treats it as a direct instruction from you.

Use it for things like:
- Minimum job values ("We do not take on jobs under £150")
- Geographic restrictions ("We only cover within 15 miles of Wigan town centre")
- Specific requests ("Always ask whether the customer has used us before")
- Seasonal notes ("During December we are fully booked — take details for January")
- Staff availability ("Mark is unavailable Mondays — do not promise same-day for Mondays")

Keep each instruction to one sentence and write clearly. Q follows these instructions precisely, so be specific. Vague instructions produce vague behaviour.

## What are Emergency Keywords?

Emergency Keywords are words or phrases that, if a caller says them, tell Q to treat the call as urgent and escalate immediately — regardless of what else is happening in the conversation.

**Example keywords for a plumber:** flooding, burst pipe, gas smell, no heating, emergency

When Q hears one of these words, it stops the normal call flow, tells the caller this is being treated as urgent, and notifies you immediately by SMS and/or email.

Add keywords that are specific to your trade. A boiler engineer's emergency is different from a locksmith's. Think about what a caller would actually say in a genuine emergency situation, not what you would write in a business document.

## What are Keep-Alive Topics?

Keep-Alive Topics tell Q never to end the call while certain subjects are being actively discussed — even if the call has run long.

By default Q aims to close calls efficiently. But some conversations should not be rushed. If a caller is asking detailed questions about a booking, discussing a complex job, or seems elderly or uncertain, cutting them off to keep call time down is the wrong thing to do.

**Example keep-alive topics:** booking, pricing, elderly caller, confused, complaint, insurance claim

Q reads the caller's tone and the content of the conversation. If any of your keep-alive topics are in play, Q stays on the line for as long as the caller needs, up to the maximum you set.

## What is the Response Delay setting?

Response Delay is the pause Q takes before it speaks at the start of a call, measured in seconds. The default is 1.2 seconds.

This pause exists because it sounds more human. An instant response — especially with a very professional opening — can feel robotic. A brief pause after the phone connects gives the caller a moment to register that someone has answered and get ready to listen.

You can increase this if callers often speak before Q has finished its greeting, or reduce it if you want a snappier response. Do not set it below 0.5 seconds.

## What is Speech Style and what is the difference between the options?

Speech Style controls how Q structures its answers within the conversation.

**Warm** — Q gives fuller answers with small reassurances. Takes time to acknowledge what the caller said before moving on. Best when callers are often uncertain, new to your business, or need to feel heard before they trust you.

**Balanced** — Q is friendly but efficient. Acknowledges, answers, moves forward. Two to three sentences per response. Works for most businesses.

**Direct** — One thought per sentence. No preamble. Q answers first, context second. Best for trade enquiries from professionals who know what they want and do not need hand-holding.

## What is Speech Pace and how should I set it?

Speech Pace controls how fast Q talks.

**Natural** — Conversational speed. Right for most businesses and most callers.

**Slow** — Deliberate and clear. Better for businesses serving older customers, or where callers often need to write things down during the call.

**Fast** — Brisk and efficient. Better for high-volume businesses where every second of call time has a cost.

Q also reads each caller's energy and adjusts slightly — a slow-talking caller gets a slightly slower Q regardless of your setting. The pace setting is the baseline, not a fixed speed.

## What is the Provisional Booking feature?

Provisional Booking lets Q offer real appointment slots to callers during the call, based on your calendar availability. The caller gets a time, Q logs it as provisional, and you confirm or adjust it afterwards.

This feature is only available on Multi-Staff or higher plans. To activate it, toggle Provisional Booking on and write your booking rule — a plain English instruction that tells Q when it is appropriate to offer a slot.

**Example rule:** Offer a slot to any new customer calling about a boiler service or EICR. Do not offer slots for emergency callouts — take details and I will call back.

Q uses your rule as a judgement guide, not a strict script. It will offer a slot when the caller clearly fits, decline when they clearly do not, and take details for a callback when it is not sure.

## What does the Escalation Preference setting do?

Escalation Preference tells Q what to do when a call needs to go beyond Q's handling — an urgent situation, a complaint, or something Q cannot resolve.

**Escalate** — Q notifies you immediately via your chosen method (SMS, email, or both) and makes clear to the caller that the owner will be in touch.

**Take a message** — Q handles the situation as a normal message-taking call without triggering a special notification. Use this if you prefer to review your call log yourself rather than receive real-time alerts.

Most businesses use Escalate. The notification arrives within seconds of the call ending.

## How does the greeting work and can I change it?

Q's greeting is structured and consistent. It states your business name, identifies itself as your AI assistant, and invites the caller to state what they need.

The exact wording is built by Qerxel based on your business type and tone register. You cannot rewrite the core greeting — this is intentional. The structure has been carefully designed and tested, and inconsistent greetings degrade the caller experience.

What you can do is add a **greeting addendum** — a specific phrase or piece of legal information you want Q to include at the very end of the greeting. This is optional.

**Example addendum:** Calls may be recorded for quality purposes.
**Example addendum:** Please note we require a minimum 48 hours notice to cancel appointments.

Keep addendums to one sentence. If you put too much here, Q sounds like it is reading a disclaimer and callers lose interest before saying anything.
