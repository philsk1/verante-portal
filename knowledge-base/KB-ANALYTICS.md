# Analytics and Intelligence

## What analytics does Qerxel provide?

Qerxel provides analytics at two levels:

**Call analytics (Data Analytics tab)** — What is happening with your inbound calls. Outcomes, lead conversion, referral tracking, volume trends.

**Schedule analytics (Calendar Intelligence)** — What is happening with your bookings. Busiest times, popular services, staff utilisation, no-show rates.

These two views together give you a picture of your whole business — the calls that come in (Answer) and the appointments that result (Schedule).

---

## What does the Data Analytics dashboard show?

**Call volume** — Total calls handled by Q over time. Daily, weekly, and monthly views. Shows whether your call volume is growing.

**Outcome breakdown** — How your calls are being classified. The proportion of lead_captured, booked, referred_out, filtered, escalated, hard_close, and spam. A healthy business has a high proportion of lead_captured and booked relative to filtered and spam.

**Lead conversion rate** — Of the calls where someone expressed interest (lead_captured), what percentage converted to a booking? This is your most important single number. A low conversion rate means leads are being lost between Q's call and your follow-up.

**Referral tracking** — How many callers have been referred to partner businesses, and which partners receive the most referrals. Helps you understand where your referral network is active.

**Revenue at risk** — Estimated value of missed or unconverted leads based on your average job value (if configured). Shows the cost of missed calls and slow follow-up.

---

## What does Calendar Intelligence show?

**Busiest periods** — Which days, times, and weeks have the highest appointment density. Helps you plan staffing, marketing, and availability.

**Popular services** — Which services are booked most often, and which generate the most revenue.

**Staff utilisation** — How much of each team member's available time is being booked. Low utilisation suggests availability can be reduced or other staff need more profile; high utilisation suggests you may need more capacity.

**Booking lead time** — On average, how far in advance do clients book? Short lead times suggest last-minute demand; long lead times suggest forward-planning clients.

**No-show and cancellation rates** — What percentage of appointments are being cancelled or no-showed? High rates may indicate appointment reminders are needed or your cancellation policy needs reinforcing.

## What is Q Intelligence?

Q Intelligence is the advanced analytics layer that cross-examines your call data and booking data together. This produces insights that neither data source could produce alone.

**Revenue Evaporation Map** — Where is revenue leaving before it reaches you? Missed calls, leads not followed up, cancelled appointments, failed bookings. Shows the gap between the money you could have earned and the money you actually earned.

**Behavioural Segmentation** — Your clients fall into three types:
- **Ritual clients** — Same service, same time, same staff, every time. These are your most predictable revenue. Protect them.
- **Explorer clients** — Trying different services each visit. Open to upselling and new offerings. High lifetime value if engaged well.
- **Recovery clients** — Returned after a gap, often following a cancellation cluster. Handle with extra care — they are on the edge of churning.

**Customer Fragility Index** — Predicts clients who are likely to stop booking before they actually do. A client who has rescheduled three consecutive times is a warning signal. The index flags these early so you can act before they disappear.

**Slot Demand Gap** — Shows which time slots have frustrated demand — many people calling and asking about a slot that was unavailable. This is suppressed revenue that is not visible in the booking log because the bookings never happened.

## How do I use the lead conversion data to improve my business?

If your conversion rate is below about 40% (fewer than 4 in 10 leads becoming bookings), here is what to check:

1. **Are you following up fast enough?** Leads go cold within hours. If Q captures a lead at 10am and you call back at 5pm, many will have already booked elsewhere.

2. **Is Q offering the booking link?** If you have online booking, enabling the booking_link after-call message converts leads automatically without needing you to call back.

3. **Are your prices competitive?** If callers are asking about price and then not booking, either Q's price guidance is off or your prices are above market.

4. **Is Q asking for commitment too hard?** If Q is too pushy on new customer calls (strict mode), callers may be put off. Try Open mode for new customers.

## How do I access Q Intelligence features?

Q Intelligence features appear in the Data Analytics tab. Some features are gated by subscription tier:

- Basic outcome analytics — available on all Answer tiers
- Revenue Evaporation Map — standard tier and above
- Behavioural Segmentation — professional tier and above
- Customer Fragility Index — enterprise tier
- Slot Demand Gap — standard tier and above

---

## How far back does the analytics data go?

Your analytics cover all calls and appointments since your account was created, subject to your data retention setting. The default retention is 90 days. If you increase retention, historical data is kept for longer.

The Calendar Intelligence search depth varies by tier:
- Entry calendar — no intelligence search
- Solo — 3 months
- Small Team — 12 months
- Growth — 24 months
- Large — all time

---

## Can I export my analytics data?

Call log data can be exported as CSV from the Account Settings page (GDPR export). This exports all call logs, caller details, and appointment records in a standard format.

Specific analytics reports cannot currently be exported as formatted PDFs or spreadsheets — this is a planned feature.

## What is the difference between all-time and recent performance in the Q Score?

The Q performance score uses a 50/50 blend:
- 50% weighted to your all-time call outcomes
- 50% weighted to your last 10 days of outcomes

This means a bad week does not collapse your score immediately (the all-time average provides stability), but a sustained period of poor performance does show up (the recent window provides sensitivity).

If you have had a busy period with unusual call types (for example, a run of spam calls following a viral social post), the recent window will show the distortion but the all-time average will keep your score stable.
